The Circle

A circle is all points in a plane that are a fixed distance from a given point in the plane. The circle has a focus known as the center of the circle.

Video Tutorial

Standard Form of the Equation of a Circle

The standard form of the equation of a circle with center at (h, k), and radius r is as follows: $$ (x-h)^2 + (y-k)^2 = r^2 $$

Example

Find the center and radius of: $ (x-5)^2 + (y+4)^2 = 27 $ The center is $(5, -4)$.
Notice how the numbers signs have been switched. This is the case for all circles due to the negative in the base equation above. To better illustrate, take a look at the figure below: $ (x-(5))^2 + (y-(-4))^2 = 9 $ Now we're going to find the radius. If $r^2 = 9$ then $r$ must be $r=\sqrt{9}$ which is $r=3$. We do not accept the negative solution to the radius because a circle's radius must be $r\ge 0$. $ (x-(5))^2 + (y-(-4))^2 = 3^2 \newline r=3 $

Fun Fact: Circles with $r=0$

Circles with a radius of $r=0$ are called point circles!
