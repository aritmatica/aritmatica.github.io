The Circle

A circle is all points in a plane that are a fixed distance from a given point in the plane. The circle has a focus known as the center of the circle.

Video Tutorial

Standard Form of the Equation of a Circle

The standard form of the equation of a circle with center at (h, k), and radius r is as follows: $$ (x-h)^2 + (y-k)^2 = r^2 $$ While a circle whose center is placed at $(0, 0)$ is as follows: $$ x^2 + y^2 = r^2 $$ Now that seems like a familiar formula, huh? Remember the Pythagorean Theorem? $$ a^2 + b^2 = c^2 $$

Example

Find the center and radius of: $ (x-5)^2 + (y+4)^2 = 27 $ The center is at $(5, -4)$. Notice how the numbers signs have been switched. This is the case for all circles due to the negative in the base equation above. To better illustrate, take a look at the figure below: $ (x-(5))^2 + (y-(-4))^2 = 9 \newline = C(5, -4) $ Now we're going to find the radius. If $r^2 = 9$ then $r$ must be $r=\sqrt{9}$ which is $r=3$. We do not accept the negative solution to the radius because a circle's radius must be $r\ge 0$. $ (x-(5))^2 + (y-(-4))^2 = 3^2 \newline r=3 $

Fun Fact: Circles with $r=0$

Circles with a radius of $r=0$ are called point circles!

Getting the N.E.W.S. of the Circle

N.E.W.S. means north, east, west, and south. To get the N.E.W.S. of a Circle we follow this format: $$ N: (h, k + r) \newline E: (h + r, k) \newline W: (h - r, k) \newline S: (h, k - r) $$

Example

Find the N.E.W.S. of: $ (x+5)^2 + (y-3)^2 = 4 $ Let us find the center first: $ (x-(-5))^2 + (y-(3))^2 = 4 \newline = C(-5, 3) $ Then let us find the radius next: $ (x-5)^2 + (y-3)^2 = 2^2 \newline r=2 $ Now that we have both the center and the radius, we can now obtain the N.E.W.S. $ N: (-5, 3 + 2) = (-5, 5) \newline E: (-5 + 2, 3) = (3, 3) \newline W: (-5 - 2, 3) = (-7, 3) \newline S: (-5, 3 - 2) = (-5, 5) $ Let us now plot the N.E.W.S. of the circle into the cartesian plane:
